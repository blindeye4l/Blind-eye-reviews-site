A Universal Perspective on a Visually Impaired World

Let's be honest. Sometimes, we all walk into open doors we didn't see coming. We all have moments where we glance away for a second and it leads to pain, pleasure, or even sometimes both.

On blindeyereviews, I explore my visually impaired perspective on the world, but with a universal view in mind, reviewing everything from technology, food, and fun to the daily challenges that connect us all.

My goal is to show that a different way of seeing things can be an eye-opening experience for everyone... Now this is how I see it!

We believe in empowering everyone with the tools and resources they need to succeed. Here are some essential accessibility organizations and services:

Be My Eyes

A free app connecting blind and low-vision users with sighted volunteers for visual assistance through live video calls.

Visit Be My Eyes

National Federation of the Blind (NFB)

The largest organization of blind people in the United States, advocating for the rights and opportunities of blind Americans.

Visit NFB

American Council of the Blind (ACB)

A national organization working to increase the independence, security, equality of opportunity, and quality of life for blind and visually impaired people.

Visit ACB

Rehabilitation Services Administration (RSA)

Federal agency providing vocational rehabilitation services to help people with disabilities achieve employment and independence.

Visit RSA

American Foundation for the Blind (AFB)

Creating a world of no limits for people who are blind or visually impaired through research, education, and advocacy.

Visit AFB

Hadley Institute for the Blind

Providing distance education and support services to people who are blind or visually impaired and their families.

Visit Hadley

VoiceOver (Apple)

Built-in screen reader for Apple devices, providing spoken descriptions of onscreen elements and navigation assistance.

Learn About VoiceOver

NVDA Screen Reader

Free, open-source screen reader for Windows, enabling blind and visually impaired people to access and interact with computers.

Visit NVDA

WebAIM

Comprehensive resources for web accessibility, including guidelines, tools, and training for creating accessible web content.

Visit WebAIM
